# Killing Joke: A History

Killing Joke are an English post-punk band formed in Notting Hill, London, in 1978. The original line-up consisted of Jaz Coleman (vocals, keyboards), Paul Ferguson (drums), Geordie Walker (guitar), and Youth (bass). They are known for their heavy, rhythmic sound which has been cited as a key influence on industrial rock and metal.

## Early Years (1978–1982)
Their debut self-titled album (1980) captured the tension of the era with tracks like "Requiem" and "Wardance". They quickly established a dedicated following with their intense live performances and apocalyptic lyrics. "What's THIS for...!" (1981) and "Revelations" (1982) continued their exploration of dub, funk, and punk.

## Commercial Success (1983–1986)
With "Fire Dances" (1983) and "Night Time" (1985), the band moved towards a more polished, gothic rock sound. "Love Like Blood", from "Night Time", became their biggest commercial hit, reaching the top 10 in the UK and expanding their audience significantly. "Brighter Than a Thousand Suns" (1986) continued this trend but with more controversy over its production.

## Shift and Separation (1987–1990)
Tensions led to line-up changes. "Outside the Gate" (1988) was controversial for its synthesizer-heavy sound, largely driven by Coleman. The band fractured but reformed for "Extremities, Dirt and Various Repressed Emotions" (1990), which saw a return to a heavier, aggressive sound and featured Martin Atkins on drums.

## Legacy and Influence
Killing Joke's influence is vast.
- **Nirvana**: The riff from "Come as You Are" was famously compared to Killing Joke's "Eighties".
- **Metallica**: Covered "The Wait".
- **Nine Inch Nails** & **Ministry**: Cite them as major industrial influences.
2003's self-titled album saw Dave Grohl on drums, cementing their status among modern rock giants.

## Recent Era
The original line-up reunited in 2008 following the funeral of Paul Raven (who had replaced Youth). They released "Absolute Dissent" (2010), "MMXII" (2012), and "Pylon" (2015), receiving critical acclaim for maintaining their intensity and relevance.  Tragically, guitarist Geordie Walker passed away in November 2023, marking a significant loss for the band and the music world.
